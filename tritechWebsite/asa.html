Certification Requirements

The process of becoming an Accredited Senior Member (ASA) is a five-to-seven year commitment. The primary requisites for senior membership include:

  • A bachelor's degree or higher from a recognized and fully accredited college or university.
  • Successful completion of the ASA Ethics Examination.
  • Successful completion of an approved USPAP course.
  • Submittal of reports demonstrative of typical work product.
  • Evidence of at least five years full-time experience within the chosen discipline.

    • Re-certification requires a minimum of 100 hours of continuing education including retaking and passing the USPAP course every five years.
